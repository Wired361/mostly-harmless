Analysis

Overview

Our goal for this project was to investigate the factors involved in improving a student's test scores. This meant a multilevel analysis of the tracked variables affecting the students and their interaction with the scores.

If we average the scores of all the students based on whether they completed test prep, we can see that students who complete test prep score higher than students that don't.

Let's start by checking some of the other columns for clues. (I did the grunt work for us so I'll just share my results) Parents are always involved in their children's lives, so their education might have some correlation with the kids' scores.




Parents

"If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of Giants."

Isaac Newton British Two Pound Coin

There does appear to be some advantage to having highly educated parents.

This makes a lot of sense. As a kid, if you have a question, first you ask Siri: "Hey, Siri, what's the square root of pi?" and while Siri calculates for the rest of time, they then ask their parents.

After seeing this plot, we can make the assumption that highly educated parents will probably know the answer, helping their kids do better on tests.

We're making progress, but if our end goal is to help students perform better, we can't very well ask their parents to go back to school in order to give their child a better test score.
