Analysis

Overview

If we average the scores of all the students based on whether they completed test prep, we can see that test prep does appear to have a positive effect on their scores.

Shocker, I know. We want to know why. More digging is required...

Let's start by checking some of the other columns for clues. (I did the grunt work for us so I'll just share my results) Parents are always involved in their children's lives, so their education might have some correlation with the kids' scores.

Parents

There does seem to be some advantage to having highly educated parents. This makes a lot of sense. If you have a question as a kid, first you ask google: "OK, Google, what's the square root of pi?"

While their poor phone calculates for the rest of time, they'd ask their parents. After seeing this plot, we can make the assumption that highly educated parents will probably know the answer, helping their kids do better on tests.
